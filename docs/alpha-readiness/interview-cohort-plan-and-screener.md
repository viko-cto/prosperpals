# ProsperPals Interview Cohort Plan and Screener

Use this file to keep the interview lane disciplined before real participant names are attached.

This artifact closes a specific blocker inside:

**`alpha-readiness / interview-evidence-and-hosted-hardening-execution`**

The blocker was not just “no sessions yet.” It was also that recruiting could drift into an easy-but-wrong cohort and later get narrated as evidence.

This plan makes that harder.

---

## 1. Why this exists

The interview lane already had a tracker and score thresholds.

What it still lacked was a concrete cohort design for:
- who each of the 12 slots should roughly be,
- how to keep budget-first and invest-first evidence balanced,
- how to avoid overfitting to friends who already like fintech,
- and what minimum screening questions should be asked before a slot is counted as valid recruiting progress.

The rule is simple:

> if a recruit does not fit one of the target slots below, they may still be interesting, but they do not automatically improve alpha-readiness evidence.

---

## 2. Elicitation methods applied

### First Principles
Used to reduce the interview lane to the actual evidence need: does Denmark-first, digital-payment-native behavior support the ProsperPals wedge without triggering trust collapse?

### Comparative Analysis Matrix
Used to avoid a lopsided cohort by forcing the plan to balance archetype, life stage, payment habits, and hosted-alpha plausibility rather than optimizing for convenience.

### Critique and Refine
Used to pressure-test the cohort against predictable founder mistakes: too many “smart finance” users, too many friendly insiders, too few trust-sensitive mainstream users, and too little evidence about who can survive a real hosted alpha.

---

## 3. Target cohort architecture

### Fixed evidence shape
- **12 total interview slots**
- **4 budget-first**
- **4 invest-first**
- **4 mixed / flex**
- **At least 8 with strong daily digital-payment behavior**
- **At least 6 plausible hosted-alpha follow-up candidates**

### Anti-bias rules
Do not let any of these dominate the cohort:
- personal friends who already know the founder story,
- people who actively trade and therefore skip the onboarding-learning wedge,
- users with no regular digital payments,
- users who are only polite and not honestly critical,
- people recruited entirely from one social cluster.

### Recruiting-source mix target
Across 12 slots, aim for:
- **max 4** from direct founder/family/friend network,
- **min 3** from second-degree referrals,
- **min 3** from external communities / student / young professional groups,
- **min 2** from people with enough discipline to plausibly join a hosted alpha and return more than once.

## 5. Slot acceptance rules

A candidate can occupy a slot only if the screener establishes enough evidence for that slot.

### Minimum required fields before a slot becomes `scheduled`
- candidate name or alias captured offline,
- assigned slot ID,
- recruitment source recorded,
- archetype hypothesis recorded,
- digital-payments fit marked yes/no,
- hosted-alpha fit marked yes/maybe/no,
- reason this person fits the slot written in one sentence.

### When to reject or reroute a candidate
Reject or reroute when:
- they clearly fit no planned slot,
- they are too far outside the Denmark-first behavioral wedge,
- they are already overly primed by the founder and unlikely to give honest reactions,
- they are a heavy-investing power user who bypasses the product’s starter value,
- or they cannot participate in a language / context that yields reliable notes.

## 6. Lightweight screener

Use these questions before scheduling.

### Required screener questions
1. **How do you currently keep track of day-to-day spending, if at all?**
2. **When you think about money apps, do you care more about staying on top of spending or learning how to grow money?**
3. **How often do you use digital payments like MobilePay, card, or Apple Pay in a normal week?**
4. **Have you ever tried investing, simulated investing, or learning about investing on your own?**
5. **Would you be open to testing an early product twice in one week if the first session goes well?**

### Mapping rules
- Strong spending answer + weak investing interest → likely **budget-first**
- Strong investing curiosity + lower budgeting motivation → likely **invest-first**
- Clear interest in both → likely **mixed**
- Rare digital-payment use → likely lower Denmark-first fit
- Willing to test twice in one week → stronger hosted-alpha fit

## 7. Batch allocation plan

Do not cluster all similar users into one batch. Each batch should generate contrast.

| Batch | Planned slots | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 01 | PP-B01, PP-I01, PP-M01 | Establish first contrast across the three behavior shapes before wording drifts. |
| 02 | PP-B02, PP-I02, PP-M02 | Confirm whether first fixes hold across higher hosted-alpha-fit users. |
| 03 | PP-B03, PP-I03, PP-M03 | Stress-test the wedge with more neutral/varied sources before optimism sets in. |
| 04 | PP-B04, PP-I04, PP-M04 | Use the final batch to pressure-test boundary cases and closing trust risk. |

### Batch stop rule
If any batch produces:
- 2 or more severe trust reactions,
- repeated inability to explain Goldie vs Fin,
- or repeated “childish / scammy” ProsperCoins reactions,

then pause the next batch until the synthesis explicitly says what changed.

---

## 8. Evidence-quality rules

### A recruit only counts as progress if:
- they are attached to a slot,
- the screener logic is documented,
- the tracker is updated,
- and the session note lands the same day once completed.

### A finished batch only counts if:
- all 3 session notes exist,
- the batch synthesis exists,
- threshold deltas are written from notes rather than memory,
- and any exclusion decision is recorded explicitly.
